Origin

The CS Supplement was founded in 2025 to address the growing need for additional resources in Purdue's computer science curriculum. This project was created to help students better understand course material and provide extra practice opportunities outside of the classroom. I (Kruz Schurz) started this project after feeling unprepared and overhwhelmed while preparing for my first computer science exam. I wanted to create a centralized platform for Purdue CS students to access course based materials and exercises to help them succeed in their studies.

Introduction

The "CS Supplement" is designed to provide additional resources and support for students enrolled in Purdue computer science courses. This includes access to supplementary materials, study guides, practice exercises to enhance learning outcomes, and primarily offer new programmers a structured way to improve their coding skills outside of regular coursework.

Features

  • Access to online tutorials, as well as video resources and recordings over content.
  • Interactive coding exercises with course content based feedback. (IN-PROGRESS)
  • Course based projects and assignments to reinforce learning.
  • Course related exercises and quizzes for exam preparation.
